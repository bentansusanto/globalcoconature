Coconut Briquette Charcoal

A briquette is a block of combustible material that is used as a fuel to start and maintain a flame. Coconut shells made into activated carbon have several advantages over those made from other materials, namely the level of hardness Which facilitate handling properties, large surface area, high absorption, produces little ash, and has a high degree of purity.

Coconut Peat Blocks

Coconut Peat is an organic material derived from coconut coir powder which is commonly used as a mixture of planting media, especially planting media in pots. Compared to other components of growing media, Coconut Peat has many advantages, one of which is being able to absorb water 10 times better than other organic materials.

Desiccated Coconut

Desiccated coconut (DC) is the result of processing coconut that is mashed by chopping or grating it without adding other ingredients and then drying it hygienically. The nutritional value of DC is quite high and resembles coconut in general. Starting from the fat content, protein fiber, vitamins and minerals can also be obtained from DC. The nutritional content is certainly good for the body to fulfill daily nutrition.

Virgin Coconut Oil

Virgin coconut oil (VCO) is coconut oil obtained by extraction or compression at a temperature of no more than 60 °Celsius, so that the resulting oil is clear like water and the nutritional content, aroma and taste of coconut are well preserved. Commonly used for cosmetic raw materials and also consumed directly as a high-calorie nutritional intake.

Copra

Copra is the dried flesh of coconuts. Copra is one of the most important coconut derivative products, because it is the raw material for making coconut oil and its derivatives. To make good copra, you need coconut that is about 300 days old and weighs about 3-4 kg.

Coconut Fiber

Coconut fiber is the outermost part of the coconut that wraps around the coconut shell. The thickness of coconut husk ranges from 5-6 cm which consists of the outermost layer (exocarpium) and inside (endocarpium). Endocarpium contains fine fibers that can be used as a rope making materials, sacks, pulp, carpets, brushes, doormats, heat and sound insulators, filters, upholstery materials seats/cars and hardboard boards. One coconut produces 0.4 kg of coir which contains 30% fiber.

Copra Meal

Copra meal is a very important feed ingredient for livestock, which is made from the remaining desiccated coconut/copra extract. With a fairly high protein content of about 16%-18%, it is suitable for use as protein-rich artemia and fish feed. How to use by sprinkling the powder on the media directly or can be dissolved then the solution is filtered and spread on the media pond / tub, to reduce feed residues that are not consumed.

Coconut oil

Coconut oil is a vegetable oil extracted from the flesh of the coconut fruit (species: Cocos nucifera). Many manufacturers have begun to use coconut oil in packaged products, and many people use it for cooking. Many products, such as fried foods, sweets, shampoos, coffee, smoothies, contain coconut oil.
